# Explain the role of preparers and approvers in the tool.

In the 2025 Compliance Risk Assessment (CRA) tool, the roles of preparers and approvers are central to the execution of the assessment within the City Risk and Controls (CRC) module. Both roles operate at the most granular level of the assessment to drive the final results used for reporting and socialization.

### **The Role of the Preparer**
The preparer acts as the **initiator of the risk assessment** and is responsible for the bulk of the manual entry and documentation. Their specific responsibilities include:

*   **Initiating the Assessment:** The preparer starts the assessment in the tool and first assigns a **business activity rating** based on their expertise and provided data kits.
*   **Assigning Ratings:** While many ratings (like publication-level ratings) are now systemically calculated, the preparer performs **qualitative assessments** at the Level 2 (L2) and Level 1 (L1) tiers. This involves using Subject Matter Expertise (SME) to manually assign ratings informed by quantitative data.
*   **Providing Rationale:** For every manually assigned L2 rating, the preparer is **mandated to provide a written justification or rationale**. The tool prevents submission until these fields are completed.
*   **Managing Issues:** Preparers have the capability to **tag or untag issues** from the systemically mapped list to reflect their specific knowledge of the GAU (Global Assessment Unit), although this does not change the systemic quantitative calculations.
*   **Documenting Conclusions:** The preparer must complete several **mandatory conclusion fields** in the residual risk section, including the business profile, inherent risk themes, and control environment themes.
*   **Validation and Submission:** Once all mandatory fields (marked with an asterisk) are completed and the validation counter reaches zero, the preparer submits the assessment, moving it to "pending approval" status.

### **The Role of the Approver**
The approver (or reviewer) is responsible for the **oversight and finalization** of the assessment. Their role involves:

*   **Reviewing Work:** The approver enters the system to review the documentation, ratings, and rationales provided by the preparer.
*   **Determining Status:** After review, the approver can either **approve** the assessment, which moves it into "completed" status, or **return it to the preparer** if any changes or additional information are required.

### **Access and Requirements**
Both preparers and approvers must request specific **entitlements** for the Risk Assessment module through City Marketplace prior to the cycle start date. # Explain all the users, maker checkers and major minor workflows in CRA application

The 2025 Compliance Risk Assessment (CRA) application operates within the **City Risk and Controls (CRC)** module and follows a structured workflow involving specific user roles and a "maker-checker" governance process.

### **User Roles**
The application defines several key participants involved in the risk assessment cycle:

*   **Preparer (The "Maker"):** The primary user responsible for initiating the assessment, performing granular execution, assigning qualitative ratings, and providing mandatory written rationales.
*   **Approver (The "Checker"):** A reviewer who evaluates the preparer's documentation and ratings to ensure consistency and accuracy.
*   **Coordinators:** These users facilitate the start of assessments for specific Global Assessment Units (GAUs) or Service Assessment Units (SAUs) and act as points of contact for guidance.
*   **Compliance Independent Assessment (CIA) Colleagues:** Internal stakeholders who use the finalized risk assessments to inform their monitoring and testing activities.
*   **Line of Business (LoB) and Global Function Leads:** Subject matter experts available to assist preparers and approvers throughout the cycle.

### **The "Maker-Checker" Process**
The application enforces a **maker-checker** control to ensure data integrity and oversight:

1.  **Drafting:** The **Preparer** (Maker) enters the tool, completes all mandatory fields, and provides qualitative SME (Subject Matter Expert) input.
2.  **Validation:** The tool includes a **"Validate" section** with a counter; the preparer cannot submit the assessment until all mandatory fields (marked with an asterisk) are completed and the counter reaches zero.
3.  **Submission:** Once validated, the preparer submits the assessment, which changes the status to **"pending approval."**
4.  **Review and Action:** The **Approver** (Checker) reviews the submission. They have two choices:
    *   **Approve:** This moves the assessment to **"completed"** status.
    *   **Return:** If changes are needed, the approver sends it back to the preparer, returning it to draft status for revision.

### **Major and Minor Workflows**
The workflows in the CRA application are categorized by the core assessment path and supplementary actions.

#### **Major (Primary) Assessment Workflow**
*   **Initiation and Business Activity Rating:** The workflow begins by selecting a **Business Activity rating** (High, Medium, or Low). This rating is assigned once and extrapolated across the entire GAU to influence publication-level ratings.
*   **Qualitative Assessment (Hybrid Approach):** Users perform a qualitative assessment at the Level 2 (L2) and Level 1 (L1) tiers. While publication-level ratings are systemically calculated, the preparer must manually assign the final L2 and L1 ratings informed by that quantitative data.
*   **Conclusion and Reporting:** The final major step is completing the **residual risk conclusion section**, which includes business profiles and thematic summaries. These results are eventually aggregated for socialization at the CBNA-GBRCC and the Board of Directors.

#### **Minor (Secondary) and Optional Workflows**
*   **Issue Tagging/Untagging:** Preparers can manually **add issues from ICAPS or untag existing ones** to better reflect the GAU's risk profile. Note that this is a "minor" workflow because it does not change the systemic quantitative calculations, though it informs the qualitative rationale.
*   **Level 3 (L3) Assessments:** The tool allows for assessments at the L3 risk level; however, this is **optional** and defaulted to "not assessed," as L3 ratings are not required by the 2025 methodology.
*   **Business Activity Adjustment:** As long as an assessment is in **"draft" status**, a preparer can return to the initial screen to adjust the Business Activity rating, which triggers a systemic recalculation of all associated publication ratings.
*   **Attachments and History:** Users can upload supporting documents in the **"Attachments"** section and view the **"Workflow History"** to see a timestamped log of who performed specific actions in the tool.

# what is  inherent risk and how does it related to CRA, explain how a  inherent risk is identified, and maintained and concluded in CRA

**Inherent risk** is a foundational component of the Compliance Risk Assessment (CRA) that, when coupled with control environment results, determines the final **residual risk**. Within the CRA framework, it serves as a key metric to inform compliance monitoring and testing activities and provides a credible challenge to the front line's risk management. For the 2025 cycle, inherent risk follows a **bottoms-up perspective**, starting with systemically calculated ratings at the publication level that are then rolled up to higher levels of the taxonomy.

### **Identification of Inherent Risk**
Inherent risk is identified through the interaction of two primary dimensions: **Severity** and **Likelihood**.

*   **Severity:** This starts with the **publication tier** (e.g., Tier 1 or Tier 2) from the Regulatory Inventory. It is then adjusted by the **Significance of Impact**—a systemically aggregated score of financial, operational, reputational, business disruption, and conduct impact data pulled from the MCA. A "very high" impact can bump the severity up one tier, while a "low" impact can bring it down.
*   **Likelihood:** This is determined by three specific factors:
    1.  **Business Activities:** Reflects the operational scale and profile of the unit; this is the **only factor manually assigned** by the assessor using an offline data kit.
    2.  **Complexity:** A systemically calculated measure of the number of distinct processes and external dependencies associated with a publication.
    3.  **Applicability:** A quantitative measure of how relevant a publication’s obligations are to the specific Global Assessment Unit (GAU).

### **Maintenance of Inherent Risk**
In the 2025 cycle, inherent risk is maintained through a **hybrid approach** that combines systemic quantification with qualitative Subject Matter Expertise (SME).

*   **Systemic Publication Ratings:** Ratings at the publication level are **100% systemically calculated** and cannot be modified by the user.
*   **Qualitative SME Assessment:** Maintenance occurs at the **Level 2 (L2) and Level 1 (L1) tiers**, where the preparer must manually assign ratings informed by the underlying quantitative data and SME knowledge. 
*   **Mandatory Rationale:** For every qualitatively assessed L2 rating, the preparer **must provide a written justification** identifying the drivers of that rating.
*   **Optional L3 Assessments:** While Level 3 (L3) risks are available in the tool, they are **not required** to be assessed and default to "not assessed" unless the user chooses to rate them.

### **Conclusion of Inherent Risk in CRA**
The inherent risk assessment is concluded through a structured roll-up and thematic documentation process:

*   **Roll-up Logic:** Manually assigned L2 ratings roll up to drive the L1 compliance risk ratings, which finally aggregate into the **overall GAU inherent risk rating**.
*   **Thematic Summary:** As part of the final "Conclusion" section of the assessment, the preparer must document **Inherent Risk Rating Themes**. This field is mandatory and requires a summary of the key drivers at the GAU level, such as specific business processes or activities that influenced the final rating.
*   **Validation:** The assessment is only considered concluded once the **"Validate" counter reaches zero**, confirming that all mandatory qualitative ratings and rationales for inherent risk have been entered.

This change is part of the firm's "target state" journey to meet **consent order deliverables** and align compliance risk management with broader enterprise control standards.

### **History and Background**
In previous assessment cycles, the CRA was performed using **assessment entities** which were manually constructed from various Global Risk and Control Assessment Units (GRCAUs). This structure was specific to the compliance function and did not always align perfectly with how other parts of the firm viewed organizational risk. For the 2025 cycle, Citi has moved to the **MCA (Manager's Control Assessment) GAU-SAU structure**. This transition ensures that all control components across the firm are assessing risk against **one common universe**.

### **The Components: GAU and SAU**
The new structure categorizes assessment units into two primary types:
*   **Global Assessment Units (GAUs):** These are the primary units for most assessments and are organized by **Line of Business (LoB) and Global Function**.
*   **Service Assessment Units (SAUs):** These units typically represent assessments at the **legal entity level** and are managed by specific coordinators responsible for those entities.

### **Key Decisions in the Structural Shift**
A fundamental decision for the 2025 cycle was to move away from **"anchoring risk"** for publication mapping. 
*   **Previous Approach:** Publications were brought into an assessment based on the "anchoring risk" of the overall publication in the Regulatory Inventory. If a publication was anchored to "Operational Risk," it was often excluded from the CRA.
*   **Target State Approach:** The CRA now utilizes **obligation mapping** from the Regulatory Inventory. For Tier 1 and Tier 2 publications, obligations are mapped to specific **Level 2 (L2) compliance risks**. This means that if any obligation within a publication—even one anchored to another risk type—is mapped to a compliance risk, that publication will now be systemically included in the CRA.

### **Integration and Effects on the CRA Process**
The move to the GAU-SAU structure has several direct impacts on how assessments are executed and integrated within the **City Risk and Controls (CRC)** tool:

*   **Granular Execution:** Preparers now find their specific assessment unit by **ID or Name** within the CRC dashboard rather than searching for legacy assessment entities.
*   **Broadened Scope:** Because the tool now pulls publications based on individual obligations rather than overall anchoring, the **span of publications** coming into an assessment may be broader than in previous years.
*   **Multi-Mapping of Publications:** A single publication may appear **multiple times** within one GAU assessment if its obligations map to different L2 compliance risks.
*   **Extrapolated Business Activity:** The **Business Activity rating** (High, Medium, or Low) is assigned once at the GAU level by the preparer and is systemically **extrapolated across all publications** within that GAU to influence inherent risk ratings.
*   **Systemic Calculations:** The structure allows for **100% systemic calculation** of publication-level ratings by pulling data from the Regulatory Inventory, MCA, and PARCUM based on the specific GAU/SAU mapping.
*   **Reporting and Socialization:** Finalized GAU results are aggregated and socialized at high-level committees, including the **CBNA-GBRCC** and the **Board of Directors**, providing a unified view of risk across the enterprise common universe.
