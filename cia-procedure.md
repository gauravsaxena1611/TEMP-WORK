# CIA Various Modules Working Together

1. Planning Units and ICRM Assessment Entity Universe

   1. PUs represent buiness or function subject to independent assessment on a stand alone basis
   2. PUs are composed of ICRM Assessment Entities (ICRM AEs) including Business org., Product Offerings, Geographies, or specific purpose
   3. ICRM AE(s) consist of GRC AUs which are the common approach for defining assessment units.
   4. IRCM AE framework is used by CRA, AML, AB& C, EWORA, ABRA, HARA

----
2. Significant Compliance Risks (SCRs)
   
   1. Use CRC-CRA module to identify SCR and determine AEs with L1-L2 Risk Categories and Tier 1-Tier 2 Risk Rating, consider external and internal factors  
   2. Consider third- party dependency planning using TP- RAP, ATPC, MCA AE-AU mapping report
   3. Identifies Enterprise wide Thematic issues 
   4. Prepare Plan to provide risk based coverage of SCRs identified

----
3. Developing the Coverage Plan

   1. Coverage is defined as the independent assessment of SCR(s) through monitoring or testing activities
   2. The Senior Manager shares an annual view of their coverage plan (monitoring and testing) with respective COs by end of Q1
   3. The Plan will be reviewed and updated with stakeholders on a periodic basis throughout the year to ensure it continues to be agile and dynamic to address evolving areas of SCR


----
4. Compliance Independent Assessment

   1. Monitoring and Testing Activities are available to Compliance Independent Assessment teams to evaluate the SCRs included in The Plan.
   2. Monitoring is ongoing, Agile and designed to identiy potential compliance weakness.
   3. Testing is periodic, restrospective and indepth assessement to identify broader compliance risks and issues
   4. Standard routines are objectives and steps applicable across businesses to drive monitoring design and content based on a specified risk

----
5. Designing and Executing Monitoring Activities

   1. Effective design of monitoring Assessment Activities is critical to evidence adequate coverage of SCR(s). It includes Objective, Procedure steps and outcome.
   2. Other factors considered are Data Privacy, Data source, applications, third party risk, consumer complaints etc.
   3. Execute the monitoring as designed and is evidenced and documented in the ICRM Monitoring module within CRC
   4. Documentatin in the form of workpapers in support of each monitoring Assessment Activity is stored in The Monitoring module in CRC
   5. The Review Manager approval is captured in CRC and represents a sign-off for: Execution of the monitoring Assessment Activity and its results

----
6. Scoping Testing Assessment Activities

   1. Review tagged to the relevant L1 and L2 Risk(s), Il as to relevant in-scope LRRP(s).
   2. Scoping is to enable teams to review prposed LRRPs; covered SCRs; Legal Entities; and objective of the Review.
   3. This results in the Review Planing Document. include Regulation Due Diligence performed to gain an understanding of the LRRPs and related business processes
   4. Review Workpaper Program (RWP) and RPD is recorded in Testing module in CRC is the system

----
7. Executing Test Assessment Activities 

   1. Determine a suitable testing approach and design appropriate test steps to assess the SCR(s) and/ or compliance, including Substantive and Controls testing strategy, 100% examination, Sampling.
   2. The workpapers are completed through the test 'execution' phase of the Review. These include workpapers evidencing an understanding of the business (e.g., flowchart, narratives), process walkthroughs, the Review Planning Document (RPD), Review Work Program (RWP) and any other workpapers prepared or obtained in support of scope defining activities

----
8. Execution Results

   1. two (2) potential outcomes from the execution of Assessment Activities are No Exceptions noted and Exceptions Noted 
   2. Exceptions are captured and summarized within the Compliance Testing and the Compliance Monitoring module in CRC
   3. Exceptions noted in either Assessment Activity can result in a new Issue to be required and raised in iCAPS or a confirmation that an Issue is not required.
   4. As needed perform complete Root Cause Analysis (RCA) , created a Compliance Monitoring Issue Report (CMIR)

----
9.  Formulating Issues

    1. Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is performed to understand why the Issue(s) occurred and to document the Issue write-up that addresses the 4Cs: Concern, Cause, Consequence, and Context. The business is then required to develop CAPs that address the root-cause of the Issue to prevent its re-occurrence.
    2. Determing Issue Severity
    3. Assign to an Issue Owner

----
10. Obtaining Corrective Action Plans

    1. Corrective Action Plan(s) must be obtained from the business for every Issue identified by ICRM.
    2. The Global Issue Management Procedure defines and sets out requirements for each of the following CAPs
       - Planning
       - Compensating
       - Remediation
       - Sustainability

----
11.  Reporting

     1. The ICRM team that has identified a new Issue must report the Issue using the Compliance Monitoring Issue Report (CMIR) which is created in CRC
     2. Within seven (7) calendar days after the Test Execution End Date the Review Manager is expected to issue a Draft Report through CRC.

----
12. Issue Validation

    1. All Issues identified by Compliance Independent Assessment are tracked through completion. Validation activities must be completed for all Issue validations in the Issue Validation Module within CRC
    2. If a Compensating CAP has been completed, the Assessor must perform validation of the CAP within 30 calendar days of the CAP Target Date to independently assess whether the Business/ Function has met all closure expectations.
    3. Failure of Issue validation must be discussed with the Issue Owner and may lead to re-opening of the Issue in iCAPS.
    4. If the Assessor determines that the Issue should be re-opened, the Assessor must complete a Compliance Independent Assessment Re-Open Report.
    5. Issue Closure Without Validation can happen due either issue avoidance or issue transfer
